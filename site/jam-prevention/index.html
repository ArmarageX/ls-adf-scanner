Practical closing notes

A scanner routine works best when it is boring in a good way. Keep one input tray area, one small prep area, and one consistent destination for finished files. If a page is fragile, curled, glossy, unusually small, or personally sensitive, slow down and decide whether the feeder is really the right path. A few seconds of preparation often prevents the longer frustration of rescanning, renaming, or hunting for a missing page later.

Before buying or changing your setup, check current product specifications, operating-system support, supported paper weights, maintenance instructions, and privacy expectations for the place where the files will be stored. Those ordinary checks are what turn an automatic feeder from a tempting gadget into a dependable paperwork habit.
